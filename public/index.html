Title 

In a land of ice and snow,
Where the winds of winter blow,
There lived a brave and noble king,
Whose praises all the bards did sing.

His kingdom was a place of peace,
Where troubles and worries did cease,
But one day, an army came,
To threaten his land with war and flame.

The king, he rallied all his men,
And rode out to the battlefield then,
The clash of swords, the thunder of hooves,
Echoed through the land, a battle that proves.

For days and nights, they fought and bled,
Until the enemy army was finally led,
To surrender to the king's command,
And peace returned to the land.

And so the king, he ruled in peace,
His people's joys he did increase,
And all the bards did sing his praise,
For he had led them through dark days.

The kingdom thrived, the people grew,
And all because the king, he knew,
That courage and honor must prevail,
To keep his kingdom safe and hale.

Read & Learn about poem types!

Read a short description about 20 poem types and select the one you like the most!

Sonnet

A 14-line poem with a specific rhyme scheme and meter, traditionally used to express love or deep emotion.

Haiku

A three-line poem with a 5-7-5 syllable count, typically focusing on nature and capturing a single moment or image.

Free Verse

A poem that does not follow a specific rhyme or meter pattern, giving the poet complete creative freedom to express their thoughts and emotions.

Narrative Poetry

A poem that tells a story, with a clear beginning, middle, and end, often featuring characters and a plot.

Ballad

A poem that tells a story through song, often featuring a refrain, and typically focused on love, betrayal, or adventure.

Epic Poetry

A long, narrative poem that typically follows a hero's journey and explores themes of love, war, and honor.

Ode

A poem that praises or celebrates a person, place, or thing, often written in a formal style.

Elegy

A poem that mourns the loss of someone or something, often written in a somber or melancholic tone.

Ghazal

A poem of Middle Eastern origin, consisting of rhyming couplets and a refrain, often focused on themes of love, loss, and separation.

Pantoum

A poem consisting of four-line stanzas in which the second and fourth lines of each stanza serve as the first and third lines of the next stanza, with the final stanza repeating the first and third lines of the first stanza.

Sestina

A poem consisting of six six-line stanzas and a three-line concluding stanza, with a specific pattern of repeating end-words.

Tanka

A five-line poem with a 5-7-5-7-7 syllable count, typically focused on nature and emotions.

Rondeau

A poem consisting of thirteen lines, with a specific rhyme scheme and repeating lines, often used for light or humorous subjects.

Triolet

An eight-line poem with a specific rhyme scheme and repeating lines, typically used for light or humorous subjects.

Acrostic

A poem in which the first letter of each line spells out a word or phrase, often used for puzzles or to convey a hidden message.

Concrete

A poem in which the visual arrangement of the words on the page forms a picture related to the content of the poem.

Epigram

A short, witty poem or saying, often used to make a pointed observation or criticism.

Ekphrastic

A poem inspired by a work of art, often focused on the emotions or thoughts the art evokes in the poet.

Prose Poetry

A poem written in prose form, without a specific meter or rhyme scheme, but still using poetic language and techniques.
